Sri Lanka Traditional Rice Recipe

Description

Sri Lankan traditional white rice, known as "samba" or "nadu," is a staple in the island's cuisine. With its long grains and fluffy texture, it pairs perfectly with various curries and sambols, absorbing spices and enhancing flavors.

Beyond its culinary role, rice holds cultural significance in Sri Lanka, often featured in festivals and rituals. It reflects the island's agricultural heritage and is central to daily meals and social customs

Ingrediants

  • 250g white rice
  • 500ml water
  • salt
  • 1-2 table spoons oil or ghee

Optinal

  • A few pandan leaves or cinnamon stick for added aroma

Steps

  1. Measure 250g of white rice using a kitchen scale or measuring cup.
  2. lace the rice in a bowl or fine-mesh strainer. Rinse under cold running water until the water runs clear, removing excess starch. This helps prevent the rice from becoming too sticky.
  3. Soak the rinsed rice in water for about 15-30 minutes. This step is optional but can help the grains cook more evenly.
  4. After soaking, drain the rice well.
  5. In a pot, combine the drained rice with approximately 500ml of water. Adjust the water if needed based on your rice variety and desired texture.
  6. Add salt to taste (optional). You can also add oil or ghee for extra flavor, if desired.
  7. Place the pot over medium-high heat and bring the water to a boil.
  8. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes. Avoid lifting the lid during cooking.
  9. After 15-20 minutes, check if the water has been absorbed and the rice is tender. If itâ€™s not done, cover and cook for a few more minutes.
  10. Once cooked, remove the pot from heat and let it sit, covered, for about 5 minutes. Then, fluff the rice gently with a fork.
  11. Transfer the rice to a serving dish and enjoy it with your favorite curries or side dishes!
