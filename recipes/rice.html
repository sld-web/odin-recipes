Sri Lanka Traditional Rice Recipe

Description

Sri Lankan traditional white rice, known as "samba" or "nadu," is a staple in the island's cuisine. With its long grains and fluffy texture, it pairs perfectly with various curries and sambols, absorbing spices and enhancing flavors.

Beyond its culinary role, rice holds cultural significance in Sri Lanka, often featured in festivals and rituals. It reflects the island's agricultural heritage and is central to daily meals and social customs

Ingrediants

  • 250g white rice
  • 500ml water
  • salt
  • 1-2 table spoons oil or ghee

Optinal

  • A few pandan leaves or cinnamon stick for added aroma
